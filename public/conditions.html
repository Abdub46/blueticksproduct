DIABETES MELLITUS

Definition of Diabetes mellitus:

A chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood glucose (sugar) levels due to defective insulin secretion, insulin action, or both. Diabetes can lead to long-term complications affecting the eyes, kidneys, nerves, and cardiovascular system if not properly managed.

Interventions:

1. Healthy Eating: Encourage balanced meals, limit sugar, refined carbs, and unhealthy fats.

2. Regular Physical Activity: Promote daily exercise appropriate to the patient’s ability.

3. Medication Adherence: Take prescribed medications consistently and as instructed.

4. Blood Sugar Monitoring: Check blood glucose regularly and report unusual readings.

5. Weight Management: Maintain a healthy weight to support blood sugar control.

6. Routine Health Checks: Regular check-ups for eyes, feet, kidneys, and blood pressure.

7. Patient Education: Teach about diabetes, its complications, and how to prevent them.


HYPERTENSION

Definition of Hypertension

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a chronic medical condition in which the force of blood against the artery walls is consistently too high, usually defined as systolic ≥140 mmHg or diastolic ≥90 mmHg. It increases the risk of heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, and other complications if left uncontrolled.

Interventions:

1. Healthy Diet: Eat more fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein; limit salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

2. Regular Physical Activity: At least 30 minutes most days, combining aerobic and strengthening exercises.

3. Weight Management: Maintain a healthy weight; even modest weight loss helps lower BP.

4. Medication Adherence: Take medicines exactly as prescribed; don’t stop without consulting a doctor.

5. Stress Management: Practice relaxation, meditation, deep breathing, and ensure adequate sleep.

6. Avoid Tobacco & Limit Alcohol: Quit smoking and drink only in moderation.

7. Regular Blood Pressure Monitoring: Check BP at home or clinic to track control.

8. Routine Health Check-ups: Screen for complications (heart, kidneys, eyes) regularly.

9. Limit Caffeine: Reduce intake if it contributes to temporary spikes in BP.

10. Patient Education: Teach about hypertension, complications, lifestyle changes, and early warning signs.

CANCER

Definition of Cancer

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and abnormal spread of cells in the body. These abnormal cells can invade surrounding tissues and, if not controlled, may spread (metastasize) to distant organs through the blood and lymphatic systems.

Interventions:

1. Early Detection and Screening Promote age- and risk-appropriate cancer screening programs (e.g., breast, cervical, prostate, colorectal) to enable early diagnosis and timely management.

2. Health Education and Awareness Educate individuals and communities on common warning signs of cancer, risk factors, and the importance of early health-seeking behavior.

3. Lifestyle Modification Encourage healthy behaviors, including a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables, regular physical activity, avoidance of tobacco products, and limitation of alcohol intake.

4. Infection Prevention and Control Support prevention and treatment of cancer-related infections (e.g., HPV, Hepatitis B and C) through vaccination, safe practices, and early treatment.

5. Timely Referral and Multidisciplinary Care Ensure prompt referral to appropriate oncology and specialized services for diagnosis, staging, and treatment using a multidisciplinary approach.

6. Treatment Adherence and Support Support patients to adhere to prescribed treatment plans, including surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy, hormonal therapy, or targeted therapy, as recommended by specialists.

7. Psychosocial Support Provide emotional, psychological, and social support to patients and their families to improve coping, mental well-being, and quality of life.

8. Nutritional Support Promote adequate nutrition to support immune function, maintain strength, manage treatment side effects, and prevent malnutrition.

9. Palliative and Supportive Care Integrate palliative care early to manage pain, symptoms, and distress, and to improve comfort and dignity throughout the disease course.

10. Follow-up and Long-term Care Encourage regular follow-up for monitoring treatment response, detecting recurrence, managing long-term effects, and supporting survivorship care.

TUBERCULOSIS

Definition of Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a chronic infectious disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis, primarily affecting the lungs (pulmonary TB) but capable of involving other organs such as the lymph nodes, bones, kidneys, and brain (extrapulmonary TB). TB is transmitted through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or speaks. Without imely diagnosis and appropriate treatment, TB can result in severe illness, long-term disability, and death, but it is both preventable and curable.

Interventions:

1. Early Detection and Diagnosis Promote early identification of TB through symptom screening, sputum examination, chest imaging, and rapid diagnostic tests to enable timely treatment initiation.

2. Prompt Initiation of Treatment Ensure early commencement of standardized anti-tuberculosis treatment regimens in accordance with national and international guidelines.

3. Treatment Adherence and Completion Support strict adherence to the full course of TB treatment to prevent treatment failure, relapse, and development of drug-resistant TB.

4. Infection Prevention and Control Implement appropriate infection control measures, including cough etiquette, proper ventilation, use of masks where indicated, and isolation of infectious cases when necessary.

5. Contact Tracing and Preventive Therapy Promote screening of close contacts and provision of preventive therapy to eligible individuals, especially children and immunocompromised persons.

6. Health Education and Community Awareness Educate patients, families, and communities about TB transmission, symptoms, treatment duration, and the importance of early care-seeking behavior.

7. Nutritional Support Encourage adequate and balanced nutrition to strengthen immunity, support recovery, and improve treatment outcomes.

8. Management of Co-morbidities Screen for and manage conditions that increase TB risk or complicate treatment, such as HIV, diabetes mellitus, malnutrition, and substance use.

9. Psychosocial Support and Stigma Reduction Provide counseling and social support to address stigma, promote mental well-being, and improve treatment adherence.

10. Monitoring, Follow-up, and Outcome Evaluation Ensure regular follow-up to monitor treatment response, manage side effects, and confirm treatment completion and cure.
